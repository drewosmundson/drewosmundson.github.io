About Me

My programming experience began in high school, where I created simple programs for my TI-84 calculator such as calculating the areas of triangles, finding missing angles, and automating the quadratic formula. I later attended the University of Minnesota, earning a degree in Computer Science with a minor in Management. During my studies, I learned how to collaborate in teams, use version control, build my own projects, and extend or modify large codebases.

To help pay for tuition, I worked at various coffee shops. After graduating, I had the opportunity to become the general manager of a Starbucks. While this was a different career path than I had originally intended, it provided valuable leadership experience and helped me grow by managing significant responsibilities. Seeking a role with better work-life balance, I transitioned to a position as a marketing analyst at Fingerhut, where I worked with databases using SQL, analyzed data, and collaborated with my team to roll out new promotions based on insights.

Currently, I work as a freelance developer, partnering with small businesses and teachers in Minnesota to develop software specifc for their needs. I have created a classroom buzzer tool similar to Kahoot for interactive learning environments and an in progress small-business friendly scheduling system.
